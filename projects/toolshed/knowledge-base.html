The Compound Layer designing

Knowledge Base MCP

The moat. Structured learnings from every build — what worked, what failed, what signals mattered. Queryable by AI and humans.

The insight

Weekly products are one-off bets. Some succeed, most fail. But every build generates learnings — about markets, about technical approaches, about what signals actually matter.

The Knowledge Base captures these learnings in a structured format that compounds. After 50 builds, this isn't a notes folder. It's a decision-making advantage that makes every future build systematically better.

What gets captured

Every other Toolshed component feeds into the Knowledge Base:

Decision patterns What types of ideas work? What red flags predict failure?
Technical learnings Which stacks work for which problems? What patterns saved time?
Market signals What actually drove revenue? What seemed promising but wasn't?
Process improvements What checkpoint types caught real issues? What was theater?
Design patterns What UI approaches worked? What can be reused?
How it's used

The Knowledge Base is an MCP server, queryable by AI and humans:

◆ Pre-build — "What do we know about this market from previous experiments?"
◆ During build — "Have we solved this technical problem before?"
◆ Post-launch — "What signals correlated with success in similar experiments?"
◆ Retrospective — "What patterns are emerging across builds?"
The compound effect

This is the moat. After 50 builds, the Knowledge Base contains:

  • 50+ decision records with outcomes
  • Patterns for what works in specific markets
  • Technical solutions battle-tested across projects
  • Design patterns proven to convert
  • Signal patterns that predict success/failure

This isn't information that can be copied. It's proprietary judgment, accumulated through real experiments, structured for retrieval.
