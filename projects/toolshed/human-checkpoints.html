Layer 2: Build Orchestration designing

Human Checkpoints

Structured pause points for scope, architecture, and implementation decisions. AI orchestrates; human approves.

The problem

AI can generate code faster than humans can review it. But without structured checkpoints, you end up with either constant interruption (slow) or rubber-stamping (dangerous).

The goal isn't human approval on every line. It's human judgment on the decisions that matter — and full AI autonomy on everything else.

The approach

An MCP server that manages the checkpoint lifecycle during a build:

Checkpoint types Scope changes, architecture decisions, external integrations, deployment gates.
Context surfacing At each checkpoint, surface what changed, why, and what the alternatives were.
Decision capture Record the human decision and reasoning — feeds into Knowledge Base.
Async-friendly Checkpoints don't block; they queue. Human reviews when ready.
Checkpoint categories

Based on early builds, these are the decisions that benefit from human judgment:

◆ Scope creep check — "This requires adding X. Proceed or defer?"
◆ Architecture fork — "Two approaches possible. Here are tradeoffs."
◆ External dependency — "This requires integrating with X. Approve?"
◆ Breaking assumption — "Original spec assumed X, but Y is true. Adjust scope?"
◆ Ship gate — "Build complete. Review before deploy?"
Status

Current: Designing checkpoint taxonomy. Running manually during builds to identify which decisions actually need human input.

Learning: Most builds need 5-10 checkpoints, not 50. The key is identifying which ones.
