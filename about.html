About

I'm Paul — a product strategist who builds things. Most recently I was Director of Product Experience Strategy at Mastercard, where I led strategy across 13 products in a $1B+ Cyber & Intelligence portfolio and grew my team from 1 to 8 people. Before product, I studied chemical engineering, which means I've always been wired to build end-to-end rather than just strategize from the sidelines.

Right now I'm learning new domains by building AI products in them. One per week. The approach is simple: pick something I don't know well, build a tool that solves a real problem in that space, and sell it. Revenue is the validation that I actually learned something useful.

I operate under a "get paid to learn" philosophy. I'd rather build a rough first version in an unfamiliar domain than study that domain for six months. The act of shipping forces clarity. Selling forces accountability. Both teach faster than research alone.

This isn't a startup. It's not freelancing either. Think of it as an incubator of one. I build products, sell them, and use what I learn to make the next build faster. Each week's work compounds into better instincts, better infrastructure, and — eventually — better products.

The pace is sustainable by design. No hustle culture. No "rise and grind." Just consistent output, week after week, with space to think and adjust.
